# GrowthCopilot — Email Sequence for Typeform Signups

> Send these to everyone who submits the Typeform "Get early access" form.
> All three emails should come from a personal email address (not a no-reply),
> and should feel like they're from a founder, not a product.

---

## Email 1 — Immediate send on signup

**Subject:** You're in — here's what's coming

Hi [first name],

Thanks for signing up for GrowthCopilot early access.

While you wait for the next features to ship — direct Mixpanel/Amplitude integrations, a daily email digest, and team sharing — the full demo is live right now at:

https://growthcopilot.streamlit.app

The fastest way to understand it: click **Demo mode** in the sidebar, then hit **Next step →** eight times. You'll walk through a complete 28-day incident arc, from "all clear" to "critical escalation" to "resolved." The whole story takes about 3 minutes.

If you have real Mixpanel or Amplitude data, you can also upload a CSV under **Connect Your Data** and see the briefing run on your own numbers.

One question while I have you: what's the one metric you check every morning that you wish would just tell you whether it needs action today?

Hit reply — I read every response.

[Your name]

## Email 2 — 5 days after signup

**Subject:** Quick question about your morning

Hi [first name],

I'm doing customer research for GrowthCopilot and I'd love 10 minutes of your time — no pitch, just genuine questions.

Specifically I'm trying to understand: what does your current morning ritual look like when something moves in your product metrics? Is it a Slack message, a dashboard crawl, a weekly review, something else?

The system I've built is designed to replace a specific kind of friction: the daily translation from "here's what changed" to "here's what to do about it." But I'm not sure how universal that friction is — or whether teams have already solved it differently.

If you're open to a quick call (15–20 min, completely informal), grab a time here: [CALENDAR_LINK]

If not, just replying to this email with your answer is genuinely useful too.

[Your name]

## Notes on tone

- Keep every email under 200 words
- Always end with a question or a single clear action — not both
- Use plain text formatting, no HTML templates — they perform worse for early-stage products and feel less personal
- Reply from your personal email, not a tool like Mailchimp, for the first 50 signups
- The goal of emails 1 and 2 is to get a reply or a calendar booking — not to "nurture" — so optimize for that, not for open rates
